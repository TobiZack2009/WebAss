Group 2 Elements

Overview of Group 2 Elements

Group 2 elements, also known as the alkaline earth metals, include Beryllium (Be), Magnesium (Mg), Calcium (Ca), Strontium (Sr), Barium (Ba), and Radium (Ra). These elements are characterized by having two electrons in their outermost energy level.

Periodic Trends

Periodic trends are patterns observed in the periodic table as you move across periods and down groups. Key periodic trends for Group 2 elements include atomic radius, density, melting and boiling points, electronegativity, ionization energy, and reactivity.

Property Trend
Atomic Radius Increases down the group
Density Generally increases down the group
Melting Point Generally decreases down the group
Boiling Point Varies without a clear trend
Electronegativity Decreases down the group
Ionization Energy Decreases down the group
Reactivity Increases down the group

Group 2 Elements

Beryllium (Be)

Atomic Number: 4

Atomic Radius: 112 pm

Density: 1.85 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1287°C

Boiling Point: 2970°C

Electronegativity: 1.57

Ionization Energy: 899.5 kJ/mol

Reactivity: Relatively low

Uses: Beryllium is used in aerospace materials, X-ray windows, and various electronic devices.

Magnesium (Mg)

Atomic Number: 12

Atomic Radius: 160 pm

Density: 1.74 g/cm³

Melting Point: 650°C

Boiling Point: 1090°C

Electronegativity: 1.31

Ionization Energy: 737.7 kJ/mol

Reactivity: Moderate

Uses: Magnesium is used in the production of lightweight alloys, fireworks, and as a reducing agent in the extraction of other metals.

Calcium (Ca)

Atomic Number: 20

Atomic Radius: 197 pm

Density: 1.54 g/cm³

Melting Point: 842°C

Boiling Point: 1484°C

Electronegativity: 1.00

Ionization Energy: 589.8 kJ/mol

Reactivity: High

Uses: Calcium is used in the production of cement and steel, as well as in biological systems for bone health and cellular processes.

Strontium (Sr)

Atomic Number: 38

Atomic Radius: 215 pm

Density: 2.64 g/cm³

Melting Point: 777°C

Boiling Point: 1382°C

Electronegativity: 0.95

Ionization Energy: 548.8 kJ/mol

Reactivity: Higher

Uses: Strontium is used in fireworks for red color, in producing ferrite magnets, and in medical applications such as bone scans.

Barium (Ba)

Atomic Number: 56

Atomic Radius: 222 pm

Density: 3.62 g/cm³

Melting Point: 727°C

Boiling Point: 1897°C

Electronegativity: 0.89

Ionization Energy: 502.9 kJ/mol

Reactivity: Very high

Uses: Barium is used in drilling fluids for oil and gas wells, in radiology for X-ray imaging, and in manufacturing glass and ceramics.

Radium (Ra)

Atomic Number: 88

Atomic Radius: 238 pm

Density: 5.5 g/cm³

Melting Point: 700°C

Boiling Point: 1737°C

Electronegativity: 0.90

Ionization Energy: 509.3 kJ/mol

Reactivity: Very high

Uses: Radium was historically used in luminous paints for watches, clocks, and instrument dials, and in cancer treatment.
